\begin{abstract}
Generating coherent natural-language descriptions from video content requires jointly modeling spatial appearance, temporal dynamics, and semantic context, a challenge that has driven the adoption of increasingly complex Transformer-based architectures. Existing methods commonly employ intermediate encoder modules to refine multimodal representations prior to decoding, introducing computational overhead that scales quadratically with the number of video segments. Moreover, caption generation is typically constrained to unidirectional left-to-right decoding, limiting access to future linguistic context. To address these limitations, we propose BiDecT, an encoder-free Transformer with bidirectional decoding for video captioning. BiDecT directly projects multimodal representations into a bidirectional decoding framework without intermediate encoding stages, reducing the dominant computational complexity from quadratic to linear. Each video is represented as a sequence of Groups of Pictures (GOPs), from which appearance, semantic, and motion features are extracted using pre-trained vision-language and video recognition models. A lightweight embedding module aligns these modalities within a shared representation space using learnable type embeddings. A backward decoder first generates a reverse caption to construct global right-to-left contextual representations, which are then incorporated by a forward decoder for final caption generation. In addition, a pseudo reverse caption strategy is adopted to prevent information leakage during bidirectional training. Experiments on MSVD, MSR-VTT, and VATEX demonstrate that BiDecT achieves state-of-the-art CIDEr scores of 138.0, 64.8, and 78.2, outperforming prior state-of-the-art approaches by 10.8, 2.8, and 5.2 CIDEr points, respectively. These results demonstrate that encoder-free bidirectional decoding provides an efficient and scalable alternative to conventional encoder-decoder architectures for video captioning.
\end{abstract}
