\begin{abstract}
Generating coherent natural-language descriptions from video content requires jointly modeling spatial appearance, temporal dynamics, and semantic context. Existing Transformer-based methods commonly refine multimodal representations through intermediate encoder modules prior to decoding, introducing computational overhead that scales quadratically with the number of video segments. Moreover, caption generation is typically constrained to unidirectional left-to-right decoding, limiting access to future linguistic context. To address these limitations, we propose the Encoder-free Bidirectional Decoder Transformer (BiDecT), which projects multimodal representations directly into a bidirectional decoding framework without intermediate encoding stages, reducing the dominant computational complexity from quadratic to linear. Each video is represented as a sequence of Groups of Pictures, from which appearance, semantic, and motion features are extracted using pre-trained vision-language and video recognition models. A lightweight embedding module unifies these features within a shared representation space. The decoding system operates bidirectionally: a backward decoder first generates a reverse caption to construct global right-to-left context, which the forward decoder then incorporates alongside the multimodal representation for final caption generation. To prevent information leakage during bidirectional training, a pseudo reverse caption strategy is further adopted. Extensive experiments on three widely used video captioning benchmarks demonstrate that BiDecT achieves state-of-the-art performance across all evaluation metrics while requiring substantially less training time than encoder-based alternatives.
\end{abstract}
