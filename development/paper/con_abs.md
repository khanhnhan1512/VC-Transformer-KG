# 6. Conclusion

This paper presented BiDecT, an encoder-free video captioning model that integrates multimodal features directly into a bidirectional decoding framework without any intermediate encoding stage. The model represents each video as a sequence of GOPs and combines appearance, semantic, and motion features through a lightweight embedding module, while a pseudo reverse caption strategy enables effective bidirectional decoding without information leakage. BiDecT achieves state-of-the-art CIDEr scores of 138.0 on MSVD, 64.8 on MSR-VTT, and 78.2 on VATEX, surpassing prior methods by +10.8, +2.8, and +5.2 points, respectively. Ablation studies on MSR-VTT confirm the contribution of each design choice: bidirectional decoding improves CIDEr by +4.52 over the unidirectional baseline; the pseudo reverse caption strategy is essential, as removing it causes CIDEr to drop to 58.36, below even the unidirectional baseline of 60.31; and modality-specific type embeddings are critical, with their removal causing the three-modality model (61.18) to underperform appearance alone (61.79). The encoder-free design further proves both faster and more effective, reducing per-epoch training time from 383.46 to 219.77 seconds compared to a variant with three encoder layers per modality while achieving higher CIDEr. Together, these findings indicate that with sufficiently rich pre-trained representations, an encoder-free bidirectional decoder design can surpass architectures that rely on intermediate encoding stages, while remaining computationally more efficient.

However, the case study in Section [$\sout{???}$ 5.7]() reveals a remaining limitation. In the analyzed failure case, the visual similarity between yawning and singing (both involving a wide-open mouth) misleads the model into substituting the less common action with one that better fits its language priors. Notably, one of ten human annotators for the same video also described the action as singing, confirming that this ambiguity is genuine rather than solely a model artifact. This finding suggests that the current multimodal features, while effective for object identification and temporal reasoning, can be insufficient for fine-grained action discrimination, and that incorporating audio-visual grounding or explicit action recognition signals represents a promising direction for future work.

# Abstract

Video captioning requires generating coherent natural language descriptions from video content, demanding an understanding of both visual appearance and temporal dynamics. Existing Transformer-based approaches commonly refine visual features through intermediate encoder layers before decoding, introducing overhead that scales quadratically with the number of input video segments, while caption generation is typically constrained to unidirectional left-to-right decoding. We propose BiDecT (Encoder-free Bidirectional Decoder Transformer), a framework that projects multimodal features directly into a bidirectional decoding system without intermediate encoding, reducing the dominant computational cost from quadratic to linear. Each video is represented as a sequence of Groups of Pictures (GOPs), from which three complementary feature types are extracted: appearance features from the image encoder of a pre-trained vision-language model, semantic features derived from the descriptions it generates, and motion features from a video classification model. A lightweight embedding module unifies these features while preserving modality identities through learnable type embeddings. A Backward Decoder generates a reverse caption to construct global right-to-left context, which the Forward Decoder incorporates alongside the multimodal representation to produce the final caption. A pseudo reverse caption strategy prevents information leakage between the two decoders during training without requiring additional annotations. BiDecT achieves state-of-the-art CIDEr scores of 138.0 on MSVD, 64.8 on MSR-VTT, and 78.2 on VATEX, outperforming prior methods by up to 10.8 points. These results demonstrate that encoder-free bidirectional decoding is a practical and effective approach for video captioning.
