# Cover Letter

```
Dear editors and reviewers,
We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "AttBiTi: Temporal-Aware Bicomplex Embeddings with Implicit Attention for Knowledge Graph Link Prediction," for consideration as a research article in Data Mining and Knowledge Discovery. This work is original, has not been published previously, and is not currently under consideration for publication elsewhere.
This manuscript is highly relevant to the readership of Data Mining and Knowledge Discovery as it advances temporal knowledge graph (TKG) embeddings through the introduction of AttBiTi, a novel bicomplex-based embedding model with implicit attention. Unlike previous approaches that rely on separate hyperbolic and quaternion embeddings, AttBiTi unifies attention-weighted temporal translation and bicompound rotational transformations into a single framework. This integration allows the model to dynamically adapt relational and temporal influences, improving both computational efficiency and interpretability—two key challenges in temporal fact prediction.
AttBiTi introduces a bicomplex-based embedding model that leverages implicit attention mechanisms to balance relational and temporal influences in TKGs. Instead of requiring separate hyperbolic embeddings, the model inherently captures hierarchical, cyclic, and evolving relational structures through a unified bicomplex transformation. Our theoretical analysis and ablation studies demonstrate the critical role of attention-weighted bicomplex transformations, showing significant improvements in expressiveness and generalization over state-of-the-art models. To validate the effectiveness of AttBiTi, we conduct extensive experiments on benchmark datasets, including ICEWS14, ICEWS5-15, GDELT, YAGO11k, and Wikidata12k, where our model consistently outperforms competing approaches, achieving up to 21% improvements in Mean Reciprocal Rank (MRR) and Hits@K metrics.
We appreciate your time and consideration of our manuscript, and we look forward to your feedback.
Sincerely, 
Thanh Le
```
