# Title: BiDecT: Encoder-Free Bidirectional Decoder Transformer for Video Captioning

## Abstract

Video captioning requires a comprehensive understanding of complex spatial-temporal dynamics and rich multimodal interactions. While recent Transformer-based models have driven significant progress, they typically rely on heavy intermediate encoders and unidirectional decoding mechanisms, thereby failing to fully exploit bidirectional semantic contexts. In this paper, we propose a novel encoder-free Bidirectional Decoder Transformer (BiDecT) for video captioning. By completely discarding the conventional intermediate encoder, our architecture directly integrates rich multimodal representations (specifically visual, semantic, and motion features extracted via pre-trained foundation models) into a unified decoding system. The proposed BiDecT consists of a Backward Decoder that captures right-to-left semantic contexts and a Forward Decoder that seamlessly attends to both the multimodal inputs and the backward hidden states to generate fluent, logical, and accurate captions. Furthermore, we adopt a pseudo-reverse caption strategy during training to effectively mitigate information leakage, and employ the Peri-LN normalization strategy to ensure gradient stability. Extensive experiments on three challenging benchmarks (MSVD, MSR-VTT, and VATEX) demonstrate that BiDecT achieves highly competitive, state-of-the-art performance, validating the effectiveness and efficiency of our encoder-free bidirectional design.

## 1. Introduction

Video captioning is a prominent and challenging task at the intersection of computer vision and natural language processing. It aims to automatically generate natural language descriptions that accurately depict the visual content and dynamic events within a video `<cite>`. Successfully bridging the modality gap requires an algorithm to not only capture fine-grained spatial objects and temporal motion but also properly align them with textual semantics. Recently, deep learning architectures, particularly those based on the attention-driven Transformer framework `<cite>`, have largely replaced traditional CNN-RNN pipelines, achieving remarkable success in multimodal representation learning.

Despite these advancements, existing Transformer-based video captioning methods still face two major limitations. First, most current frameworks heavily rely on complex intermediate Encoder modules to process visual features before passing them to the language decoder. This conventional encoder-decoder paradigm can incur high computational costs and potentially create an information bottleneck that dilutes the rich semantics extracted by modern pre-trained vision-language models. Second, traditional decoders generate sentences strictly in a left-to-right (L2R) autoregressive manner. This unidirectional approach inherently ignores the right-to-left (R2L) future context, which is crucial for establishing coherent sentence structures and accurate semantic relationships. Although a few recent studies have explored bidirectional decoding to capture these contexts `<cite>`, applying such architectures efficiently without heavy encoders remains underexplored. 

To address these challenges, we propose a novel encoder-free Bidirectional Decoder Transformer, termed **BiDecT**. Diverging from prior works, we entirely remove the intermediate encoder block. Instead, we directly map a rich set of multimodal representations (comprising static visual features and high-level semantic features extracted from I-frames via BLIP-2 `<cite>` and SRoBERTa `<cite>`, alongside temporal motion features from MViTv2 `<cite>`) into a common embedding space using specialized Type Embeddings. Our core architecture consists solely of a Backward Decoder and a Forward Decoder. The Backward Decoder generates the sequence in reverse, thereby compressing the holistic video semantics into a robust R2L hidden context. Subsequently, the Forward Decoder utilizes a supplementary cross-attention mechanism to jointly attend to the multimodal input features and the backward context, ultimately generating highly descriptive and logical captions. To stabilize the deep network training, we strictly adopt the Peri-LN strategy. More importantly, to prevent the well-known information leakage problem commonly found in bidirectional decoding tasks, we follow prior studies `<cite>` by adopting a *pseudo-reverse caption* training strategy. This approach shuffles the available ground-truth captions within each video, thoroughly preventing the forward decoder from trivially exploiting exact future tokens and forcing the model to learn genuine bidirectional contextual dependencies.

The main contributions of this work are summarized as follows:
*   We propose BiDecT, a novel encoder-free video captioning framework that directly integrates multimodal features into a Bidirectional Decoder system, significantly reducing architectural redundancy while maximizing the representation power of pre-trained foundation models.
*   We design a robust multimodal embedding module equipped with specific Type Embeddings to seamlessly fuse visual, semantic, and motion representations without semantic confusion.
*   Extensive experiments demonstrate that our proposed BiDecT achieves state-of-the-art or highly competitive performance on three challenging benchmark datasets (MSVD, MSR-VTT, and VATEX). Both quantitative metrics and qualitative analyses highlight the model's superior ability to generate semantically rich and contextually accurate video captions.
