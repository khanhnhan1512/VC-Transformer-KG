# 7. Conclusion

In this paper, we propose a novel encoder-free Bidirectional Decoder Transformer (BiDecT) architecture for the video captioning task. The core innovation of BiDecT lies in its paradigm shift towards maximizing the exploitation of bidirectional semantic contexts during the caption generation process. Unlike conventional frameworks, our approach completely discards the intermediate Encoder block. Instead, it directly integrates multimodal features (comprehensively capturing visual, semantic, and motion representations) into a unified Bidirectional Decoder system. This streamlined design allows the model to effectively leverage both left-to-right and right-to-left contextual guidance, leading to more accurate and semantically rich descriptions. 

Extensive experiments conducted on three challenging video captioning benchmarks, namely MSVD, MSR-VTT, and VATEX, solidly demonstrate the effectiveness of our proposed method. The empirical results prove that despite the removal of the encoder module, BiDecT achieves highly competitive performance against recent state-of-the-art methods across multiple evaluation metrics.

In the future, we plan to further improve our method in two promising directions. First, we intend to replace the current MViTv2-small model with larger and more powerful networks to extract richer and more robust motion features. Second, we aim to design an intermediate multimodal fusion module capable of optimally synthesizing the visual, semantic, and motion characteristics into a more unified and refined representation, which will provide even stronger guidance for the decoder modules.
